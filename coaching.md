# Coaching

This page summarizes my findings about coaching. The ability to coach its followers is critical for a leader. 


Coaching and Mentoring: The ability to guide and develop individuals by providing feedback, support, and opportunities for growth. This includes understanding individual strengths and weaknesses, providing guidance, and facilitating learning and development.

## Key Competencies
Goal-Orientation: Coaching is centered around specific goals or objectives that the coachee wants to achieve. These goals can be related to personal development, career advancement, skill enhancement, or any other area where the coachee seeks improvement.

Active Listening and Questioning: Coaches employ active listening skills to understand the coachee's perspectives, challenges, and aspirations. They ask thought-provoking questions to stimulate reflection and promote deeper insights and self-discovery.

Feedback and Accountability: Coaches provide constructive feedback to help coachees identify areas for improvement and recognize their progress. They also hold coachees accountable for the actions and commitments they set for themselves, ensuring they stay on track toward their goals.

Coaches work with clients to help them find their own answers and strategies for success.

## Difference between coaching and mentoring
It is important to note that coaching is distinct from mentoring or counseling. While coaching focuses on the present and future, mentoring involves sharing knowledge and guidance based on the mentor's expertise, and counseling addresses emotional or psychological issues.


# The seven essential coaching questions
* “What's on your mind?”
* “And what else?”
* “What's the real challenge here for you?”
* “What do you (really) want (from me)?”
* “How can I help?”
* “If you're saying yes to this, what are you saying no to?”
* “What was most useful for you?”
