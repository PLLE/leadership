
## Goals of Agile Leadership

* Agile development process --> SCRUM
* Agile mindset --> Servant Leadership
* Agile tools & methods
* Innovation
* Resilience


Agile leadership recognizes that traditional hierarchical and command-and-control structures may not be effective in today's complex and uncertain world. Instead, it focuses on creating a more flexible and empowered organizational culture that enables teams to innovate, respond quickly to changes, and deliver value to customers.

Here are some key principles and characteristics of agile leadership:

* Vision and purpose: Agile leaders provide a clear vision and purpose to guide their teams. They articulate the organization's goals and objectives and help employees understand how their work contributes to the larger picture.

* Empowering teams: Agile leaders empower teams by giving them autonomy, trust, and decision-making authority. They create an environment where teams are encouraged to take ownership of their work, experiment, and learn from failures.

* Collaboration and communication: Agile leaders foster a collaborative culture where cross-functional teams work together to solve problems and deliver results. They encourage open and transparent communication, both within teams and across the organization.

* Iterative and incremental approach: Agile leaders embrace an iterative and incremental approach to work. They promote the use of short feedback loops, frequent iterations, and continuous improvement. This allows for early validation, adaptation, and course correction based on customer and stakeholder feedback.

* Servant leadership: Agile leaders serve their teams by removing obstacles, providing resources, and facilitating the team's success. They support the growth and development of individuals within the team and create an environment that encourages learning and innovation.

* Adaptability and flexibility: Agile leaders are comfortable with ambiguity and change. They are responsive to market dynamics, customer needs, and emerging opportunities. They encourage experimentation and are willing to adjust plans and strategies based on new information.

* Continuous learning: Agile leaders promote a culture of continuous learning and improvement. They encourage experimentation, provide opportunities for learning and development, and foster a mindset of reflection and adaptation.

Overall, agile leadership is about creating an environment that enables teams to work collaboratively, adapt quickly to changes, and deliver value in a complex and uncertain business landscape.
